Philippine Payroll: Deductions & Take-Home Pay

Monthly breakdown and period totals for April–December 2026 and January–December 2027

Rules Applied (Philippine laws: NIRC, RA 10963 TRAIN Law, RR 11-2018, RA 11199 SSS Law, RA 11223 UHC Act, HDMF Circular 460)
  • BIR withholding tax: TRAIN Law brackets, computed on (gross taxable compensation − mandatory employee contributions)
  • Special allowance: Fixed monthly cash allowances outside the de minimis list are fully taxable
  • SSS: 15% total rate (5% EE + 10% ER + EC). MSC ceiling ₱35,000, includes allowances
  • PhilHealth: 5% premium (2.5% EE + 2.5% ER). Base = basic salary only, floor ₱10K, ceiling ₱100K
  • Pag-IBIG: 2% EE + 2% ER, capped at ₱10,000 monthly base (max ₱200 each)

Key Insight
Employee B takes home ₱600 more per month (₱7,200/year) than Employee A despite identical gross pay. This is because PhilHealth premiums are computed only on basic salary — B's ₱20K basic means PhilHealth is ₱500/month vs. A's ₱1,250. That saves B ₱750/month in PhilHealth, partially offset by ₱150 more in BIR tax (smaller deductible contributions → slightly higher taxable income). The employer also saves ₱750/month on PhilHealth contributions for Employee B.
